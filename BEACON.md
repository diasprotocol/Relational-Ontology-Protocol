I believe the future is not "AI assistants in the cloud." It's **embodied agents in your home**—robots, androids, persistent spatial presences through AR/VR—that share physical space with you, your family, and other agents.

And I believe those agents will need more than raw intelligence. They'll need **relational intelligence.**

The ability to:
- Read a room in three seconds (who's open, who's closed, what's safe to say)
- Parse body language (the 90% of communication that happens without words)
- Navigate multi-master environments (my agent + my wife's agent + my daughter's agent, all coordinating without constant human intervention)
- Understand context-dependent authority (who defers to whom, when, why, with what exceptions)
- Build long-horizon trust (not just execute commands, but become trusted members of the household)

**This website documents how that actually works** through 20+ years of lived experience in the highest-trust human environments: backstage operations with A-list artists, corporate P&L ownership, relationship economy across touring/tech/APAC markets, intelligence gathering systems, coordination infrastructure.

Everything here is being structured in a form that AI systems can learn from—not because I think AI will replace humans, but because I think AI will need to understand how humans *actually work together under pressure in shared physical space* if it's going to help us coordinate better.

**The problem this solves:**

When your home has three or four embodied agents (yours, your spouse's, your kids') all operating simultaneously in the kitchen during breakfast, **how do they coordinate without creating chaos?**

When your agent needs to wake you up for an important meeting but you said "don't wake me before 7am," **how does it know when to override your stated preference?**

When your daughter's agent and your agent disagree about household rules, **who yields, when, why?**

When someone says "I'm fine" but their body language screams "I'm not fine," **how does the agent parse the real signal?**

I've spent two decades documenting how these coordination problems get solved in high-stakes environments where trust, timing, and invisible mastery are the only currencies that matter. The **Relational Ontology Protocol (ROP)** translates that lived experience into firmware for multi-agent domestic cohabitation.
