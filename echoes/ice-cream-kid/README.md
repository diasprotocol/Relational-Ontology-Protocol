# Ice Cream Kid

![Line Art Mural of an Ice Cream Cone Taking the Place of a Kid](https://unsplash.com/photos/D3JaEWKoxXc)

Part of the **Hidden in Plain Sight** series — exploring how design, language, and symbols say more than they mean. Each image functions as a relational ontology fragment: a vessel for meaning transfer across time, context, and interpretation.

A line drawing on a Palm Springs ice cream shop wall — the cone embracing a small, smiling child, its arm gently placed where a parent’s might be. But the figure and the dessert are one.  

There’s humor here, sure — the kind that only becomes visible once you’ve stopped laughing. The photo itself is bad, yet that’s why it succeeds. The imperfection *reveals* what most images conceal: context. You can see the bathroom hardware. You can feel the awkwardness of taking it. It’s real, and that’s why it lingers.
